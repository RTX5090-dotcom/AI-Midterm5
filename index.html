Improving the Accessibility in Harlem

An AI-Based Redesign of Montefiore Square Park
Focused on Solving the Lack of Seating

About the Project

Project Title:
Improving Accessibility in Montefiore Park Through AI

Goal:
The goal of this project is to redesign Montefiore Square Park to improve accessibility, comfort, and usability by addressing the lack of seating.

Essential Question:
How can artificial intelligence be used to improve accessibility and comfort in public parks?

The Problem

Montefiore Square Park currently lacks sufficient seating and shaded rest areas. This creates a real-world accessibility issue for visitors.

Elderly individuals, families, and people with mobility challenges often struggle to find places to sit and rest, limiting their ability to fully use the park.

Site / Context

Movement: Visitors tend to stay near areas with seating and avoid sections without rest options.

Comfort: Lack of shade and seating reduces comfort, especially during warm weather.

Accessibility: Without seating, the park is difficult to use for elderly and disabled visitors.

Community Need: The Harlem community requires more inclusive and accessible public spaces.

Before & After Comparison

Before

The current park layout lacks sufficient seating and shaded areas, limiting accessibility and comfort.

After (AI Redesign)

The redesigned park introduces shaded seating huts, providing accessible and comfortable resting areas for all visitors.

Design Options

Option 1: Add more benches throughout the park.

Option 2: Add shaded seating structures.

Option 3: Use AI to optimize placement of shaded seating huts.

Final Redesign Proposal

The final redesign introduces shaded seating huts strategically placed throughout the park. These structures combine seating with overhead coverage to improve both comfort and accessibility.

Artificial intelligence is used to determine optimal placement based on visitor movement, high-traffic areas, and environmental conditions such as sunlight exposure.

This ensures seating is placed where it is most needed, maximizing usability and impact.

Reflection / Conclusion

What AI Helped With:
AI helped generate design ideas, analyze park usage patterns, and visualize the redesigned park layout.

What Required Human Judgment:
Human judgment was necessary to identify the problem, understand community needs, and select the most effective solution.

This project demonstrates that AI is a powerful tool, but meaningful design decisions require human insight.

Project Information

This project was created as part of an AI course to explore how artificial intelligence can improve accessibility and usability in public spaces within Harlem.
