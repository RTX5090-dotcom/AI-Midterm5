Improving the Accessibility in Harlem

An AI-Based Redesign of Montefiore Square Park
Focused on Solving the Lack of Seating

About the Project

Project Title:
Improving Accessibility in Montefiore Park Through AI

Goal:
The goal of this project is to redesign Montefiore Square Park to improve accessibility, comfort, and usability by addressing the lack of seating.

Essential Question:
How can artificial intelligence be used to improve accessibility and comfort in public parks?

The Problem

Montefiore Square Park currently lacks sufficient seating and shaded rest areas. This creates a real-world accessibility issue for visitors.

Elderly individuals, families, and people with mobility challenges often struggle to find places to sit and rest, limiting their ability to fully use the park.

Site / Context

Movement: Visitors move throughout the park but tend to stay in areas with seating.

Comfort: Lack of shade and seating reduces comfort, especially during warm weather.

Accessibility: Without rest areas, the park is less usable for elderly and disabled visitors.

Community Need: The local Harlem community requires more inclusive and comfortable public spaces.

Before & After Comparison

Before

The current park has limited seating and no shaded rest areas.

After (AI Design)

The redesign introduces shaded seating huts, improving accessibility and comfort.

Design Options

Option 1: Add more benches throughout the park.

Option 2: Add shaded seating structures.

Option 3: Use AI to optimize placement of shaded seating huts.

Final Proposal

The final redesign introduces shaded seating huts placed throughout the park using AI analysis.

AI was used to determine optimal locations based on visitor movement, high-traffic areas, and environmental factors such as sunlight.

This solution improves accessibility, increases comfort, and ensures that all visitors can fully enjoy the park.

Reflection / Conclusion

What AI Helped With:
AI helped generate design ideas, analyze park usage, and visualize the redesigned space.

What Required Human Judgment:
Human judgment was needed to identify the problem, understand community needs, and choose the best solution.

This project shows that AI is a powerful tool, but human decision-making is essential.
